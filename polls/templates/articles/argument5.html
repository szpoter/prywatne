New Flights and Railroad Connections from Szczecin

Szczecin is making significant strides in transportation development, introducing new rail and air connections to improve the city’s accessibility and boost convenience for both residents and tourists. With high-speed Pendolino services to Warsaw and additional flight routes from Szczecin-Goleniów Airport, the city is becoming a key travel hub in northwestern Poland.

High-Speed Rail: Szczecin to Warsaw in Just Over 4 Hours

In January 2025, PKP Intercity officially launched a new direct Pendolino service connecting Szczecin Główny with Warszawa Wschodnia. The journey now takes just 4 hours and 20 minutes, making it one of the fastest rail connections between the northwest and the capital.

The Pendolino departs Szczecin Główny at 6:00 AM, reaching Warsaw at 10:20 AM. On the return trip, the train leaves Warszawa Wschodnia at 5:00 PM, arriving in Szczecin at 9:20 PM. Key stops along the route include Stargard, Poznań Główny, and Warszawa Centralna, ensuring access to major cities along the way. This service has been praised for its comfort, speed, and reliability, attracting both business and leisure travelers.

Modernization and New Flight Connections at Szczecin-Goleniów Airport

Szczecin-Goleniów Airport is undergoing a significant transformation to accommodate rising passenger numbers. Renovation of the terminal began in March 2025, focusing on expanding the departure halls (Schengen and Non-Schengen zones) to 3,500 m² and the arrival hall to 1,000 m². Additionally, the airport plans to increase the number of security checkpoints from 2 to 5, improving the flow of passengers during peak hours.

Among the newly introduced connections, flights to Kraków began in April 2025, providing a convenient domestic link between Szczecin and Małopolska. These flights, operated twice weekly, have already become a popular choice for both tourists and business travelers. Furthermore, Ryanair launched a new connection to Dublin in February 2025, catering to the growing Polish diaspora in Ireland. LOT Polish Airlines has also expanded its service to Warsaw, now offering up to 25 flights per week, ensuring better flexibility and connectivity for passengers.

Impact of New Connections

The new rail and air connections are expected to significantly enhance Szczecin’s role as a transportation hub, fostering tourism and business opportunities. The Pendolino service, in particular, has been hailed as a game-changer, drastically reducing travel time and offering a competitive alternative to driving or flying. This service strengthens ties between Szczecin and Warsaw, benefiting both commuters and occasional travelers.

Meanwhile, the improved Szczecin-Goleniów Airport and its expanded network of flights are set to boost the region's economy. The addition of domestic and international routes enhances Szczecin’s appeal as a destination for both leisure and business travelers, encouraging growth in sectors such as tourism, trade, and real estate.

A Bright Future for Szczecin’s Connectivity

The ongoing investments in Szczecin’s transportation network underscore the city’s commitment to modernization and accessibility. With faster and more convenient travel options by rail and air, Szczecin is poised to strengthen its position as a major hub in Poland and beyond. These developments not only improve mobility but also create new opportunities for economic and cultural exchange, securing Szczecin’s place on the map as a dynamic and forward-thinking city.
