Kolumba Street is Open Now

After years of construction and planning, Kolumba Street in Szczecin is officially open to traffic. This vital thoroughfare, which has been under renovation to modernize its infrastructure, now features wider lanes, improved pedestrian walkways, and enhanced public transport access. Kolumba Street serves as a critical connection between the city center and the industrial areas of Szczecin, making its reopening a major milestone for residents and businesses alike.

Key features of the renovation include state-of-the-art drainage systems to prevent flooding, energy-efficient LED street lighting, and new landscaping that aligns with Szczecin's green city initiative. Local businesses along Kolumba Street are already reporting an uptick in customers due to the improved accessibility.

City officials celebrated the reopening with a ribbon-cutting ceremony, where Mayor Piotr Krzystek emphasized the importance of this project in enhancing urban mobility and economic growth. Residents have praised the changes, especially the dedicated cycling paths that encourage eco-friendly transportation.

Kolumba Street is expected to become a model for future infrastructure projects in Szczecin, setting a high standard for quality and sustainability.
